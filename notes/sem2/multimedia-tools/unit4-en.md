# UNIT 4 – Animation in Multimedia

# Introduction to Animation

Animation is one of the most exciting and attractive elements of multimedia. Animation creates the illusion of movement by displaying multiple images rapidly one after another.

Human eyes cannot easily detect very fast image changes. Because of this, when many images are shown quickly, our brain feels that objects are moving.

This technique is called animation.

Animation is used in:
- Movies
- Video games
- Advertisements
- Websites
- Mobile apps
- Educational videos
- Social media content

Without animation, modern multimedia systems would feel static and less interactive.

---

# Real Life Example of Animation

Examples of animation:
- Cartoon shows
- Anime
- Marvel movie CGI
- Instagram GIF stickers
- Game character movement
- Animated logos
- Website loading effects

For example:
When a character runs in a game, many images of different running positions are displayed rapidly, creating movement illusion.

# Persistence of Vision

Persistence of vision is the scientific principle behind animation.

Human eyes retain image for a fraction of second even after image disappears.

This creates illusion of continuous motion.

---

# Real Life Example

When a fan rotates quickly:
- Blades appear blurred

This happens because of persistence of vision.

---

# Principles of Animation

Animation follows several principles to create realistic movement.

These principles were introduced by Disney animators.

---

# 1. Squash and Stretch

Objects change shape during movement.

Example:
- Bouncing ball stretches while moving fast.

---

# Image Representation

```md
![Squash and Stretch](images/squash-stretch.png)
```

Suggested Image Link:
https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/01/Squash_and_stretch.svg

---

# 2. Anticipation

Small preparation movement before main action.

Example:
Before jumping:
- Character bends knees first.

---

# 3. Timing

Timing controls speed of movement.

Good timing creates realistic animation.

---

# 4. Slow In and Slow Out

Movement starts slowly, becomes fast, then slows again.

Makes movement natural.

---

# 5. Follow Through

Different body parts continue moving after main motion stops.

Example:
Hair continues moving after person stops running.

---

# 6. Exaggeration

Movements are exaggerated to make animation more expressive.

Used heavily in cartoons.

---

# Pixel Depth and Its Effect

Pixel depth is also called:
```text
Color Depth
```

It determines number of colors available.

Higher pixel depth:
- Better color quality
- More realistic animation

---

# Examples of Pixel Depth

| Pixel Depth | Colors |
|---|---|
| 8-bit | 256 colors |
| 16-bit | 65,536 colors |
| 24-bit | 16 million colors |

---

# Effect on Storage

Higher pixel depth:
- Increases quality
- Increases file size

---

# Real Life Example

Modern animated movies use 24-bit color depth for realistic visuals.

---

# Effect of Image Size on Quality and Storage

Image size affects:
- Animation quality
- Storage requirement

Large images:
- Better quality
- More storage needed

Small images:
- Lower quality
- Faster loading
