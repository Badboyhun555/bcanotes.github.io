# Introduction to Video in Multimedia

Video is one of the most powerful and attractive components of multimedia. Video combines:
- Images
- Motion
- Audio
- Text
- Effects

to create realistic and interactive communication.

Unlike static images, video can show movement, emotions, actions, and real-life situations. Because of this, videos are heavily used in:
- Movies
- YouTube
- Online classes
- Gaming
- Advertisements
- Social media
- Video conferencing

Modern multimedia systems depend greatly on video technology because humans understand moving visual content faster than plain text.

---

# Definition of Video

Video is a sequence of continuously displayed images called frames, usually combined with audio, to create motion and visual communication.

---

# Real Life Examples of Video

- Video advertisements
- CCTV recordings

# Importance of Video in Multimedia

Video plays a major role in multimedia systems because it:
- Improves understanding

- Improves communication

# Interlacing and Non-Interlacing

These are methods of displaying video frames.

---

# Interlacing

Interlaced video displays:
- Odd lines first
- Even lines later

This method was developed to save bandwidth.

---

# Working of Interlacing

Screen is divided into:
- Odd scan lines
- Even scan lines

Displayed alternately.

---

# Advantages of Interlacing

- Saves bandwidth
- Works on older TV systems

---

# Disadvantages of Interlacing

- Flickering
- Motion blur
- Lower quality

---

# Real Life Example

Old CRT televisions used interlaced display.

---

# Image Representation of Interlacing

```md
![Interlaced Video](images/interlaced-video.png)
```

Suggested Image Link:
https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0c/Interlace_example.svg

---

# Non-Interlacing (Progressive Scan)

Non-interlacing displays all lines continuously in one sequence.

Also called:
```text
Progressive Scan
```

---

# Advantages of Non-Interlacing

- Better quality
- Smooth motion
- No flickering

---

# Disadvantages

- Requires more bandwidth

---

# Real Life Example

Modern:
- LED TVs
- Smartphones
- Monitors

use progressive scan.

---

# Difference Between Interlacing and Non-Interlacing

| Interlacing | Non-Interlacing |
|---|---|
| Odd-even lines separately | Full frame together |
| Lower quality | Better quality |
| Older technology | Modern technology |
| Saves bandwidth | Uses more bandwidth |

# Digital Video Compression

Digital videos consume huge storage.

Compression reduces video size.

---

# Why Compression is Needed

Without compression:
- Videos become extremely large
- Internet streaming becomes difficult

---

# Types of Compression

1. Lossless Compression
2. Lossy Compression

---

# Lossless Compression

No quality loss.

File size reduction is limited.

---

# Lossy Compression

Some data removed to reduce size significantly.

Most modern videos use lossy compression.

---

# Real Life Example

YouTube compresses uploaded videos to save bandwidth.
