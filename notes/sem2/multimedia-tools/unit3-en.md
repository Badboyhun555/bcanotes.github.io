# What is Sound?

Sound is a form of energy produced due to vibrations.

These vibrations travel through medium such as:
- Air
- Water
- Solid objects

Human ears detect these vibrations and brain interprets them as sound.

---

# Digital Sound

Computers cannot understand natural analog sound directly.

Therefore:
- Analog sound is converted into digital form

This process is called:
- Digitization of sound

---

# Real Life Example

When we record voice using mobile:
- Microphone captures analog sound
- Device converts it into digital audio

---

# Attributes of Sound

Sound has several important properties called attributes.

Main attributes:
1. Frequency
2. Amplitude
3. Pitch
4. Volume
5. Timbre
6. Duration

---

# 1. Frequency

Frequency means number of vibrations per second.

Measured in:
```text
Hertz (Hz)
```

---

# Example

```text
100 Hz = 100 vibrations per second
```

---

# Importance of Frequency

Frequency determines:
- High sound
- Low sound

---

# Real Life Example

- Baby voice → High frequency
- Drum sound → Low frequency

# 2. Amplitude

Amplitude represents height of sound wave.

It determines loudness of sound.

---

# High Amplitude

- Loud sound

---

# Low Amplitude

- Soft sound

---

# Example

- Explosion → High amplitude
- Whisper → Low amplitude

# 3. Pitch

Pitch tells whether sound is:
- High
- Low

Pitch depends on frequency.

---

# Example

- Female voice → Higher pitch
- Male voice → Lower pitch

---

# 4. Volume

Volume means loudness level of sound.

Measured in:
```text
Decibels (dB)
```

---

# Example

| Sound | Approx Volume |
|---|---|
| Whisper | 20 dB |
| Conversation | 60 dB |
| Traffic | 90 dB |

---

# 5. Timbre

Timbre is quality or uniqueness of sound.

It helps distinguish sounds.

---

# Example

Same musical note played on:
- Guitar
- Piano

sounds different due to timbre.

---

# 6. Duration

Duration means how long sound lasts.

---

# Example

- Short beep
- Long ringtone

---

# Mono Sound

Mono means Monophonic sound.

Mono sound uses:
```text
Single audio channel
```

All sound comes from one direction.

---

# Characteristics of Mono Sound

- Single channel audio
- Simple recording
- Smaller file size

---

# Advantages of Mono Sound

- Easy processing
- Less storage required
- Good for voice recordings

---

# Disadvantages of Mono Sound

- Less realistic
- No directional effect

---

# Real Life Example

Examples:
- Old radio systems
- Walkie-talkie communication

# Stereo Sound

Stereo means Stereophonic sound.

Stereo uses:
```text
Two audio channels
```

- Left channel
- Right channel

This creates realistic sound experience.

---

# Characteristics of Stereo Sound

- Two-channel audio
- Better realism
- Directional sound effect

---

# Advantages of Stereo Sound

- Immersive experience
- Better quality
- Realistic audio

---

# Disadvantages of Stereo Sound

- Larger file size
- More processing required

---

# Real Life Example

Examples:
- Music systems
- Headphones
- Movies
- Gaming

---

# Difference Between Mono and Stereo Sound

| Mono | Stereo |
|---|---|
| Single channel | Two channels |
| Simple sound | Realistic sound |
| Small size | Larger size |
| Less immersive | More immersive |

# Sound Depth

Sound depth is also called:
```text
Bit Depth
```

Bit depth determines amount of information stored for each sound sample.

---

# Importance of Sound Depth

Higher sound depth means:
- Better quality
- More accurate sound

---

# Common Sound Depths

| Bit Depth | Quality |
|---|---|
| 8-bit | Low quality |
| 16-bit | CD quality |
| 24-bit | Professional quality |

---

# Real Life Example

Music studios use:
- 24-bit audio

for high-quality recording.

# MP4 FORMAT

MP4 stands for MPEG-4 Part 14.

MP4 supports:
- Audio
- Video
- Subtitles

---

# Features of MP4

- Multimedia container format
- High compression
- Good quality

---

# Advantages of MP4

- Supports audio and video
- Internet friendly
- Smaller size

---

# Disadvantages of MP4

- Compression may reduce quality slightly

---

# Real Life Example

YouTube videos commonly use MP4 format.

---

# Difference Between WAV, MP3 and MP4

| WAV | MP3 | MP4 |
|---|---|---|
| Uncompressed | Compressed | Multimedia format |
| Large size | Small size | Medium size |
| Best quality | Good quality | Audio + Video |
| Professional use | Music sharing | Video platforms |

---

# Sound Compression

Compression reduces audio file size.

Two types:
1. Lossless Compression
2. Lossy Compression

---

# Lossless Compression

No data loss.

Example:
- FLAC

---

# Lossy Compression

Some data removed to reduce size.

Example:
- MP3
